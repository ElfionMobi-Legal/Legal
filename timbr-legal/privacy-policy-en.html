3. Information we collect

3.1 Information you provide

  • Voice recordings. Audio you capture in-app for analysis. You should only record your own voice (or voices you have a lawful right to record).
  • Onboarding / profile preferences. For example voice-range calibration (male/female/unspecified), improvement goal, and optional age range. These are used for scoring calibration and plan tone—not for advertising profiles.
  • Support communications. If you email us, we process your email address and message content to respond.

3.2 Information created by the App and our servers

  • User identifiers. On first use we typically create an anonymous authentication session (e.g., via Supabase Auth). That ID links your scans, plans, and subscription status. Optional Sign in with Apple / Google may be offered later to upgrade the same account.
  • Analysis outputs. Overall and dimension scores, potential, headlines, tips, raw acoustic metrics (stored for product quality / Pro features), plan content, and plan progress.
  • Technical request metadata. Necessary server logs such as timestamps, request IDs, approximate IP for security/rate limiting, and error codes. We do not intentionally log raw audio in application logs.

3.3 Purchases and entitlements

If you subscribe to Timbr Pro, payment is processed by Apple or Google. We receive entitlement/status information (and related subscription metadata) through RevenueCat and/or store APIs so we can unlock Pro features. We do not receive or store your full payment card number.

3.4 Device preferences & optional analytics

  • On-device preferences: language, theme (light/dark/system), onboarding completion, and similar settings via local storage.
  • Optional Firebase Analytics / Crashlytics (when configured in a release build): aggregated usage events, device model, OS version, app version, crash stacks, and instance identifiers as processed by Google under their terms.

Audio retention commitment: Voice files are stored temporarily (private object storage such as Cloudflare R2) for analysis, then deleted after processing. Lifecycle rules provide an additional automatic delete window (for example within about 24 hours). We do not operate Timbr as a permanent voice recording library.

4. How we use information

  • To analyze your recording and display scores, tips, and a training plan
  • To calibrate scoring using the preferences you select
  • To enforce free-tier limits and unlock Pro features
  • To restore purchases and keep subscription status accurate
  • To secure the service (authentication, rate limiting, abuse prevention)
  • To fix bugs, measure reliability, and improve the product
  • To respond to support requests and meet legal obligations

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your voice recordings to train third-party foundation models for unrelated products unless we clearly disclose a change and obtain any consent required by law.

5. Legal bases (EEA/UK and similar regimes)

Where GDPR-style rules apply, we process data under one or more of: performance of a contract (providing the App you requested); legitimate interests (security, product improvement, fraud prevention—balanced against your rights); consent where required (e.g., certain analytics or optional permissions such as microphone); and legal obligation when we must retain or disclose information.

6. How voice processing works

Recordings are uploaded to our backend (Cloudflare Workers with temporary object storage and a processing queue). Acoustic features are extracted and scored on our infrastructure. Results and plans are stored in our database (Supabase/Postgres). Optional future features (for example speech-to-text for pace/filler analysis) may involve additional processors under data-processing terms; we will update this Policy if material processors change.

Not medical advice. Timbr is for training and entertainment. It is not a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, or cure any condition.

7. Sharing with service providers

We share data with vendors who process it on our instructions to operate Timbr:

  • Cloudflare — API hosting, temporary audio storage, queues, security
  • Supabase — authentication and database
  • RevenueCat — subscription entitlement management
  • Apple / Google — app distribution, billing, and (if used) Sign in with Apple / Google
  • Google Firebase (optional) — analytics and crash reporting

These providers may process data in the United States or other countries. Where required, we rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms offered by those providers (such as Standard Contractual Clauses).

We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect rights and safety, or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale (with notice where required).

8. Retention

  • Audio: deleted after analysis (plus short lifecycle backup window)
  • Scores, plans, profile: retained while your account remains active and as needed to provide the service
  • Subscription records: retained as needed for accounting, fraud prevention, and legal compliance
  • Support emails: retained as needed to resolve your request

Uninstalling the App removes local device data but does not automatically erase server-side records tied to your user ID. Use in-app Delete account (when available) or contact us as described below.

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, or port personal data, to object to certain processing, and to withdraw consent. You may also lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

Microphone: You can deny or revoke microphone permission in system settings; scoring will not work without it.

Marketing: We do not currently run third-party ad networks inside Timbr.
